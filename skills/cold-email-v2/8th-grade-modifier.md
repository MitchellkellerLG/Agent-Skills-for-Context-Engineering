# 8th Grade Reading Level Modifier

## Purpose

Enforce simple, clear language that reads at an 8th grade level or below (Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level ≤ 8.0). This modifier works WITH the cold-email-v2 framework to ensure copy is accessible and punchy.

---

## Reading Level Requirements

**Target: Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level ≤ 8.0**

- Average sentence length: ≤ 15 words
- Complex words per 100 words: ≤ 5
- Use simple, everyday language
- One idea per sentence

**Test your copy at:**
- hemingwayapp.com (free, instant scoring)
- readable.com (batch testing)

---

## Sentence Structure Rules

### Rule 1: One Idea Per Sentence

**❌ Complex:**
> "We help companies scale their outbound operations without adding headcount by leveraging AI-powered data enrichment tools that streamline the prospecting process and improve targeting accuracy."

**✅ Simple (8th grade):**
> "We help you grow your outbound team without hiring more people. We use AI tools to find better prospects faster."

**Change:** 1 sentence → 2 sentences, 28 words → 22 words

---

### Rule 2: Use Active Voice

**❌ Passive:**
> "Your pricing page was reviewed by our team and it was determined that upgrades could be increased through video content."

**✅ Active:**
> "We looked at your pricing page. Videos could help more people upgrade."

---

### Rule 3: Avoid Subordinate Clauses

**❌ With clauses:**
> "If you're trying to scale your sales team, which is something most companies do at this stage, then we might be able to help you figure out how to do that without hiring more SDRs, which can be expensive."

**✅ Direct:**
> "Want to grow your sales team without hiring more SDRs? We can help."

---

### Rule 4: Max 2 Commas Per Sentence

**❌ Too many commas:**
> "We built a platform that rewards renters for paying on time, so you can increase NOI, reduce chasing, and improve tenant relationships."

**✅ Simplified:**
> "We reward renters who pay on time. You get higher NOI and less chasing."

## Reading Level Examples

### Example 1: Wise Men Copy

**❌ Original (Grade 10.5):**
> "Fools believe they possess comprehensive knowledge. Intelligent individuals learn from their own experiences and mistakes. Wise individuals learn from the experiences and mistakes of others."

**✅ 8th Grade (Grade 7.2):**
> "Fools think they know it all. Smart people learn from their own mistakes. Wise people learn from other people's mistakes."

---

### Example 2: Exit Strategy Copy

**❌ Original (Grade 11.3):**
> "The majority of agency proprietors aspire to execute a liquidity event eventually, however the operational framework cannot function autonomously without their direct involvement."

**✅ 8th Grade (Grade 6.8):**
> "Most agency owners want to sell someday. But the business can't run without them."

---

### Example 3: Value Prop

**❌ Original (Grade 12.1):**
> "We facilitate the optimization of outbound prospecting methodologies through the implementation of AI-enhanced data enrichment protocols that enable your organization to achieve superior targeting precision without proportional headcount expansion."

**✅ 8th Grade (Grade 7.9):**
> "We help you find better prospects using AI. You get more meetings without hiring more people."

## QA Checklist Addition

Add this as **Item #12** to the cold-email-v2 QA checklist:

### 12. Reading Level Check

**✅ Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level ≤ 8.0**
- Test at hemingwayapp.com
- If grade level > 8.0 → Simplify language
- Replace jargon with everyday words
- Break long sentences into shorter ones
- Use active voice

**✅ Average sentence length ≤ 15 words**
- Count words in each sentence
- If average > 15 → Break sentences apart
- Aim for 10-12 words per sentence

**✅ No complex words**
- If hemingway highlights words in purple → Replace them
- Use the substitution table above
- Read aloud - if you stumble, simplify

## Quick Fixes for High Reading Levels

### Fix 1: Break Long Sentences
- Find sentences >20 words
- Break at natural pause points
- Use periods, not commas

### Fix 2: Replace Jargon
- Scan for business buzzwords
- Use substitution table
- Ask: "Would my 13-year-old nephew understand this?"

### Fix 3: Use Contractions
- "You are" → "You're"
- "We have" → "We've"
- "They would" → "They'd"
- More conversational = lower grade level

### Fix 4: Cut Adverbs and Adjectives
- "Highly innovative" → "New"
- "Extremely valuable" → "Valuable"
- "Significantly improved" → "Improved"

### Fix 5: Use Numbers, Not Words
- "Thirty days" → "30 days"
- "Three times" → "3x"
- "Four point seven" → "4.7x"

## Before & After (Full Email)

### Before (Grade 11.2, 89 words):
```
Subject: Strategic partnership opportunity

Greetings Marcus,

I wanted to reach out because I noticed your organization has been experiencing substantial growth. It seemed apparent that you're endeavoring to cultivate your client acquisition capabilities without proportionally expanding your team infrastructure.

We've collaborated with comparable agencies and facilitated a 4.7x enhancement in their qualification processes through implementation of our proprietary automation platform.

Would you be amenable to scheduling a brief consultation to explore potential synergies?
```

### After (Grade 7.4, 62 words):
```
Subject: Quick question

Marcus - saw you're growing fast.

Have you figured out how to get more clients without hiring more people?

We helped agencies like yours get 4.7x better at finding the right leads. All automated.

Worth a quick chat?
```

**Changes:**
- "Strategic partnership opportunity" → "Quick question"
- "Greetings" → direct to name
- "experiencing substantial growth" → "growing fast"
- "endeavoring to cultivate" → simple question format
- "proportionally expanding" → "hiring more"
- "comparable agencies" → "agencies like yours"
- "facilitated a 4.7x enhancement" → "4.7x better"
- "qualification processes" → "finding the right leads"
- "proprietary automation platform" → "automated"
- "amenable to scheduling a brief consultation" → "worth a quick chat?"

**Result:** 11.2 → 7.4 grade level, 30% shorter, infinitely clearer

## Key Reminder

**Simple ≠ Stupid**

8th grade reading level doesn't mean:
- ❌ Talking down to people
- ❌ Oversimplifying the offer
- ❌ Losing sophistication

It means:
- ✅ Respecting their time
- ✅ Making it scannable
- ✅ Getting to the point fast
- ✅ More people understand = more replies

The best cold emails are clear, not clever.
